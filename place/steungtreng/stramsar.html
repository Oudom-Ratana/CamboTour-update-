Stung Treng Ramsar Site

Stung Treng Province, Cambodia

📍 Along the Mekong River, 20–60 km north of Stung Treng city
🌿 14,600 hectares | Ramsar Convention (1999)

Mekong River's Wetland Treasure

The Stung Treng Ramsar Site is one of the most important wetlands in Southeast Asia, designated under the Ramsar Convention in 1999. Covering over 14,600 hectares of the Mekong River and its surrounding flooded forests, grasslands, and sandbars, it provides critical habitat for a vast diversity of fish, birds, reptiles, and mammals.

The site is particularly famous for its irrawaddy dolphins (a small population still survives), Siamese crocodiles, giant catfish, and the endangered Mekong giant salmon carp. Birdwatchers can spot the rare sarus crane, masked finfoot, river tern, and many species of herons, egrets, and kingfishers.

The best way to explore the Ramsar site is by boat tour, which takes you through the flooded forest (especially impressive during the wet season from July to December). Visitors can also visit local fishing communities, learn about sustainable fishing practices, and enjoy the peaceful, untouched beauty of the Mekong. The area is also close to the Sopheakmit Waterfall and Preah Rumkel village, making it easy to combine several experiences.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Take a boat tour through the flooded forest (July–December)
  • • Birdwatching – look for sarus cranes, river terns, and masked finfoots
  • • Visit local fishing villages and learn about traditional livelihoods
  • • Try to spot Irrawaddy dolphins (best from December to May)
  • • Combine with Sopheakmit Waterfall and Preah Rumkel village

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Litter – protect the fragile wetland ecosystem
  • • Fish or hunt in protected zones
  • • Disturb nesting birds or dolphin habitats
  • • Swim in deep channels (strong currents)

💡 Pro tip: The best time for birdwatching and dolphin spotting is the dry season (December–May). For flooded forest scenery, visit during the wet season (July–December). Boat tours can be arranged in Stung Treng city or through local guesthouses. Expect to pay $20–40 for a half‑day boat tour. Bring binoculars, a camera, sun protection, and insect repellent.
