Preah Rumkel & Sopheakmit Waterfall

Stung Treng Province, Cambodia

📍 35 km northeast of Stung Treng city
🌊 Mekong River & Sopheakmit Waterfall

Mekong River Rapids & Ethnic Village Life

Preah Rumkel is a remote village located on the banks of the Mekong River, just a short boat ride from the Sopheakmit Waterfall – a series of spectacular rapids and cascades that are particularly impressive during the dry season when the water is clear and turquoise. The area is part of the Ramsar wetland site, rich in biodiversity.

The village is home to the Lao and Khmer communities, who live in traditional stilt houses. Visitors can stay in basic homestays, take boat trips through the flooded forest (during wet season), and explore the nearby Koh Phdao and Koh Preah islands, which are important bird sanctuaries.

The Sopheakmit Waterfall (also known as Preah Rumkel Waterfall) is a series of rapids stretching over 1 km. The name “Sopheakmit” means “meeting of friends”. Unlike typical vertical falls, the water cascades over wide granite shelves, creating many shallow pools perfect for swimming. The backdrop of the Mekong and the surrounding forest makes it one of the most beautiful and peaceful spots in northeastern Cambodia.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Swim in the clear, turquoise rapids of Sopheakmit
  • • Take a boat trip to Koh Preah and Koh Phdao islands (bird sanctuaries)
  • • Stay overnight in a traditional homestay in Preah Rumkel village
  • • Explore the flooded forest during the wet season (July–December)
  • • Watch the sunset over the Mekong River

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Litter – keep the Mekong clean
  • • Swim in strong currents (only swim in designated safe pools)
  • • Disturb nesting birds on the protected islands
  • • Visit during the rainy season (July–October) – the waterfall becomes dangerously strong

💡 Pro tip: The best time to visit is the dry season (November–May) when the water is clear and the rapids are safe for swimming. From Stung Treng city, hire a motorbike or tuk‑tuk (about 1 hour). You can also take a boat from the O’Svay pier. Homestays cost $10–15 per night including meals. Bring insect repellent, a flashlight, and cash (no ATMs nearby).
