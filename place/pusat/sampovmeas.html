Sampov Meas Island (Koh Sampov Meas)

Pursat Province, Cambodia

📍 Pursat River, central Pursat city
🛕 Boat‑shaped man‑made island

Sacred Golden Boat Island

Sampov Meas Island (Koh Sampov Meas, or “Golden Boat Island”) is a boat‑shaped, man‑made island sitting in the middle of the Pursat River. It is one of the most beloved cultural and spiritual landmarks in Pursat city[reference:0].

Local legend says a ship sailed into the river, became stranded, and over time froze into mud, forming the island. The name “Sampov Meas” (Golden Boat) honors this story, and the island has been reshaped to resemble a ship[reference:1].

At its heart stands the Preah Ang Chao Sen temple, where locals come to pray, make wishes, and celebrate major festivals such as Khmer New Year and Visakha Bochea[reference:2]. The island is considered a sacred place where a holy spirit is believed to dwell, and it is said to have never flooded, no matter how high the river rose[reference:3].

Today, the island is beautifully landscaped with gardens, walking paths, sculptures, and a small cultural village displaying Khmer art. It’s a perfect spot for an afternoon walk, a picnic, or simply relaxing by the river while watching local life unfold[reference:4].

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Walk the peaceful paths and enjoy the river views
  • • Visit Preah Ang Chao Sen temple and make an offering
  • • See the “Cultural Village” with traditional Khmer art displays[reference:5]
  • • Watch local ceremonies during Khmer New Year and Visakha Bochea[reference:6]
  • • Take a scenic boat ride on the Pursat River

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Wear revealing clothing (shoulders/knees covered when visiting the temple)
  • • Litter or damage the gardens and sculptures
  • • Disturb worshippers who come to pray and light incense
  • • Enter restricted or closed areas of the island

💡 Pro tip: The island is open daily from 6 AM to 6 PM[reference:7]. It is best visited late afternoon or early evening to avoid the tropical heat and catch the golden light over the river. The island is free to enter, and a small donation at the temple is appreciated. From the capital, take a bus to Pursat (about 3 hours) and then a tuk‑tuk to the riverside[reference:8].
