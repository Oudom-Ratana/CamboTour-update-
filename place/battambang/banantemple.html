Banan Temple (Phnom Banan)

Banan District, Battambang Province

📍 25 km southwest of Battambang city
☀️ Nov — Mar (Cool Season)

History & Architecture

Banan Temple (also known as Phnom Banan) is a stunning 11th-century Angkorian temple built on a 400-meter-high hill. Constructed during the reign of King Udayadityavarman II (1050–1066 AD), it is often called "mini Angkor Wat" because its five sandstone towers (prasats) resemble the famous Angkor Wat, but on a smaller, more intimate scale.

To reach the sanctuary, visitors climb 358 stone steps flanked by nagas (serpent balustrades) – a steep but rewarding ascent. The temple offers breathtaking panoramic views of the Sangker River winding through endless rice paddies, palm trees, and traditional Cambodian villages.

The site includes a laterite wall, a long gallery, and two libraries. Some lintels and pediments are beautifully carved with scenes from Hindu mythology, though many have been damaged over time. Unlike the crowded Angkor complex, Banan Temple remains peaceful and off-the-beaten-path – perfect for sunset or early morning visits.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Climb the 358 ancient steps for incredible hilltop views
  • • Explore the five sandstone prasats and carved lintels
  • • Watch sunrise or sunset over the Sangker River valley
  • • Visit the nearby cave temple (Phnom Chhnork) at the base
  • • Combine with a bamboo train or Ek Phnom temple tour

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Climb or sit on the ancient stone towers
  • • Touch or remove any carvings or artifacts
  • • Wear revealing clothing (shoulders/knees must be covered)
  • • Use drones without permission from local authorities
  • • Leave rubbish on the temple grounds

💡 Pro tip: Bring water and wear sturdy shoes – the stairs are steep. Arrive before 8 AM to avoid heat and have the temple almost to yourself.
