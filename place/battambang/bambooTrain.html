Bamboo Train (Norry) – Ek Phnom

Ek Phnom District, Battambang Province

📍 9 km southwest of Battambang city
☀️ Nov — Mar (Cool & Dry Season)

History & Experience

The Bamboo Train – locally known as “Norry” – is a unique and thrilling mode of transport built from a simple bamboo platform placed on two bogie wheels, powered by a small gasoline engine. Originally created by locals to move goods and people along the neglected railway tracks left from the French colonial era, it has become a symbol of Cambodian resilience and creativity.

Located in Ek Phnom district, this iconic ride offers an unforgettable journey through lush rice paddies, rural villages, and scenic countryside. The 7–8 km route takes you across wooden bridges, past water buffalos, and through authentic farming landscapes. When two bamboo trains meet on the single track, the one with fewer passengers gets dismantled quickly — a fascinating ritual that shows the resourcefulness of local operators.

Today, the Bamboo Train is a must-do activity for travelers seeking an authentic, offbeat adventure. The ride lasts about 30–45 minutes round trip, with stops to admire views and interact with friendly villagers. It's a perfect blend of history, engineering ingenuity, and pure fun.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Ride the bamboo train (round trip ~ $5–10 per person)
  • • Watch the “dismantling” ritual when two trains meet
  • • Capture stunning photos of rural Cambodia & rice fields
  • • Visit nearby Ek Phnom temple & local villages
  • • Interact with friendly train operators & villagers

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Stand up or lean out while the train is moving fast
  • • Touch the engine or moving mechanical parts
  • • Litter on the tracks or rice fields
  • • Force the train to exceed passenger capacity (max ~15 people)
  • • Negotiate aggressively — fares are already fair & support locals

💡 Pro tip: Go around 4:00 PM to enjoy golden hour views and cooler breeze. Bring sunglasses and a scarf for dust.
