Bat Cave – Sunset Spectacle

Phnom Sampeau, Battambang Province

📍 12 km southwest of Battambang city
☀️ Year-round (best Nov–Mar)

Natural Wonder

Every evening just before sunset, millions of wrinkle-lipped bats emerge from a cave on the western face of Phnom Sampeau Mountain. This spectacular "bat eruption" creates a living river of wings that twists and spirals into the dusk sky, sometimes stretching for over 30 minutes.

The bats spiral higher and higher, forming a dark ribbon against the orange and purple sunset before dispersing across the countryside to feed for the night. It's one of Southeast Asia's most unforgettable wildlife spectacles, attracting photographers, nature lovers, and travelers from around the globe.

The cave itself is part of the Phnom Sampeau complex, which also includes a golden stupa, Buddhist shrines, and the Killing Caves memorial. Arrive early to secure a good spot at the western viewpoint, then stay to watch the show as the sun dips below the rice paddies.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Watch the bat eruption from the western viewpoint
  • • Arrive 45 min before sunset for a good spot
  • • Bring binoculars for an up-close view
  • • Combine with Killing Caves & golden stupa visit
  • • Capture stunning sunset + bat photos

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Use flash photography – it disturbs the bats
  • • Stand directly under the cave entrance
  • • Throw objects or shout at the bats
  • • Litter on the mountain or viewpoint

💡 Pro tip: Best viewing months: November to March (dry season). Arrive by 5:15 PM. Bring insect repellent and a light jacket – it gets cool after sunset.

🦇 One of the world's largest bat colonies – a truly magical sunset experience. Please respect the wildlife and keep noise to a minimum.
