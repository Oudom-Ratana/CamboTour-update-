Kep National Park

Kep Province, Cambodia

📍 5 km west of Kep town center
🌿 Established: 1993 | 50 sq km

Coastal Hill Forest with Ocean Views

Kep National Park is a protected area of evergreen and semi‑evergreen forest covering the hills just behind Kep town. It offers a cool, shaded escape from the tropical heat, with well‑marked hiking trails that lead to stunning viewpoints over the Gulf of Thailand and the nearby islands (Koh Tonsay, Koh Pos, and others). The park is home to a rich variety of wildlife, including long‑tailed macaques, dusky langurs, hornbills, and many species of butterflies and reptiles.

The main trail is a 2.5 km loop that starts at the park entrance near the famous Kep Butterfly Farm. The path is paved in parts but can be steep and slippery after rain. Several lookout points offer panoramic views, and you can also explore side trails to abandoned French villas and a small waterfall. The park is also home to the Kep National Park Pagoda, a serene hilltop temple.

Unlike the flat countryside, the hills provide a pleasant microclimate – cooler and breezier than the coast. It's a perfect half‑day activity for nature lovers and photographers. The park is free to enter, though a small donation is appreciated for trail maintenance. You can easily combine a morning hike with a swim at Kep Beach or a seafood lunch at the famous Crab Market.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Hike the 2.5 km main loop (easy to moderate, 1–2 hours)
  • • Enjoy panoramic views of the Gulf of Thailand and islands
  • • Visit the hilltop pagoda and abandoned French villas
  • • See wildlife – monkeys, hornbills, butterflies
  • • Combine with the Kep Butterfly Farm (at the entrance)

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Litter – carry all trash back with you
  • • Feed or harass the monkeys
  • • Hike alone at dusk or after dark (monkeys can be aggressive)
  • • Wear flip‑flops (the trail can be slippery; wear sturdy shoes)

💡 Pro tip: Start early (7–8 AM) to avoid the midday heat. Bring water, insect repellent, and a camera. The trail is clearly marked, but a local guide at the entrance can provide interesting history ($5–10). The park is free, but a small donation for maintenance is encouraged. After the hike, enjoy fresh crab at the nearby Kep Crab Market.
