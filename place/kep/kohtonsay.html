Rabbit Island (Koh Tonsay)

Kep Province, Cambodia

📍 4.5 km southwest of Kep town (20‑30 min by boat)
🏝️ 2 sq km | White sand beaches

Idyllic Island Escape

Rabbit Island (Koh Tonsay) is the most popular island getaway from the coastal town of Kep. Despite its name, you won't find rabbits – the name is said to come from the island's shape, which resembles a rabbit from certain angles. The island is a tranquil paradise of white‑sand beaches, shallow turquoise waters, and dense casuarina and palm forests.

Development is deliberately low‑key. Accommodation consists of basic but charming wooden bungalows run by local families. There is no electricity during the day (generators run from around 6 PM to 10 PM), and no Wi‑Fi – the perfect place to disconnect. Fresh seafood is caught daily and grilled right on the beach.

Activities are simple: swimming, snorkeling (bring your own mask), beachcombing, hiking to the island's small interior hill for views, or just relaxing in a hammock. The island is just a short boat ride from Kep, making it an easy day trip or an idyllic overnight stay. It's family‑friendly, safe, and a wonderful contrast to the busy crab market and hills of Kep.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Swim in calm, clear waters (great for kids)
  • • Snorkel (bring your own gear – limited rentals)
  • • Hike to the hilltop for island views
  • • Eat fresh grilled squid, fish, and prawns on the beach
  • • Stay overnight in a basic bungalow ($10–25/night)

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Litter – the island has no waste management; take trash back
  • • Expect luxury or reliable electricity (it's rustic)
  • • Use credit cards (bring cash in Cambodian riel or US dollars)
  • • Stay past 5 PM if you don't have accommodation (last boat returns around 4‑5 PM)

💡 Pro tip: Boats depart from Kep Pier (near the Crab Market) when full or by charter. Round trip: $10–12 per person. The last boat back is around 4–5 PM. For a true escape, stay one night – you'll have the beach almost to yourself in the evening. Bring insect repellent, sunscreen, a flashlight, and a power bank.
