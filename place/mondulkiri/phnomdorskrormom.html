Phnom Doh Kromom

Sen Monorom, Mondulkiri Province

📍 3 km north of Sen Monorom town center
☀️ Year-round (best Nov–Apr dry season)
🌄 Mondulkiri's best sunset viewpoint

Sacred Guardian Hill of Sen Monorom

Phnom Doh Kromom (ភ្នំដោះក្រមុំ), also known as Yok Sros Phlom ("Grandmother & Grandfather" hills), is a pair of twin hills overlooking the provincial capital of Sen Monorom. Rising to approximately 800 meters (2,600 feet) in elevation, it is one of Mondulkiri's most iconic and sacred landmarks.[reference:0]

For the indigenous Bunong people, Phnom Doh Kromom is a powerful spiritual site – home to guardian spirits that protect Sen Monorom and the surrounding lands. At the summit, you'll find a small shrine (neak ta) where locals leave offerings of fruit, incense, and money to pray for health, safety, and good fortune.[reference:1] Both hills have Buddhist shrines at their peaks, making this a living place of worship where ancient animist traditions blend with Buddhist practice.[reference:2]

The southern hill features a wooden viewing platform that offers breathtaking panoramic views of Sen Monorom town, rolling green hills, and endless highland forest. It is widely considered the best sunset spot in Mondulkiri – the golden hour light transforms the landscape into a sea of amber and gold.[reference:3] The site is free to visit, easily accessible, and a perfect introduction to the natural and spiritual beauty of Cambodia's eastern highlands.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Watch the famous sunset from the wooden viewing platform
  • • Visit the Buddhist shrines and Bunong neak ta shrine at the summit
  • • Enjoy panoramic views of Sen Monorom town and rolling hills
  • • Take a short, easy hike to the top (10–15 minutes)
  • • Combine with a visit to Samot Cheur ("Ocean of Trees") 5km north

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Litter or damage the shrine and religious statues
  • • Make loud noise near the shrine area (respect worshippers)
  • • Remove any offerings left at the neak ta shrine
  • • Drive vehicles to the summit (walking path only)

💡 Pro tip: Arrive at 5:00 PM to secure a good spot for sunset. Bring a light jacket – it gets cooler at the top. The hill is only a 5–10 minute drive from Sen Monorom center by motorbike or tuk-tuk. Entry is free, but a small donation at the shrine is appreciated. Combine with a visit to Bousra Waterfall or an elephant sanctuary for a full day.
