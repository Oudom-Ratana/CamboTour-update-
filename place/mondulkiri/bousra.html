Bousra Waterfall

Mondulkiri Province

📍 43 km northeast of Sen Monorom town
☀️ Dec — Apr (Dry season, clearest water)

Mondulkiri's Most Spectacular Waterfall

Bousra Waterfall (also spelled Bou Sra) is the largest and most famous waterfall in eastern Cambodia. Located deep in the lush jungles of Mondulkiri province, it cascades over three main tiers, dropping a total of nearly 40 meters. The widest tier stretches over 100 meters across, creating a thunderous curtain of water that crashes into a deep, cool pool perfect for swimming.

The waterfall is sacred to the indigenous Bunong people, who believe spirits live in the falls. Visitors can swim in the lower pool (lifeguards are on duty), hike to the upper viewpoints, and explore the surrounding protected forest, which is home to gibbons, hornbills, and elephants. The area has well‑maintained wooden walkways, changing rooms, and picnic shelters.

The journey to Bousra is an adventure – a winding red‑clay road through Mondulkiri's rolling hills, past elephant sanctuaries, coffee plantations, and Bunong villages. It's best visited during the dry season (December to April) when the water is clear and the road is passable. The wet season (May–October) makes the waterfall even more powerful, but swimming can be dangerous and the road muddy. Bousra is a must‑see for nature lovers and photographers.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Swim in the natural pool at the base of the falls
  • • Hike to the upper tiers for panoramic views
  • • Picnic at designated shelters (bring your own food)
  • • Visit nearby Bunong villages and learn about their culture
  • • Combine with a visit to an elephant sanctuary

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Litter – carry all trash back with you
  • • Use soap or shampoo in the swimming pool
  • • Climb on slippery rocks near the main drop
  • • Visit during heavy rain (dangerous flash floods)

💡 Pro tip: Rent a motorbike in Sen Monorom ($10–15/day) or hire a taxi ($35–45 round trip). The road is red dirt – go slowly, especially after rain. Bring water, snacks, and a towel. Entry fee is about $2–3 per person. Go early (8–9 AM) to avoid crowds.
