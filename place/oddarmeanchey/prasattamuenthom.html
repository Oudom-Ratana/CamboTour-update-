Prasat Ta Muen Thom

Oddar Meanchey Province (Cambodian-Thai border)

📍 ~140 km northwest of Siem Reap, near the border
☀️ Nov — Mar (Cool & dry season)

Remote Angkorian Border Temple

Prasat Ta Muen Thom is a late 11th‑century Angkorian temple built during the reign of King Udayadityavarman II (and later completed by Suryavarman II). It sits directly on the Cambodian‑Thai border in the Dângrêk Mountains, in Oddar Meanchey province. The temple is part of a group of three sanctuaries (Ta Muen Toch, Ta Muen, and Ta Muen Thom), with Thom being the largest and most impressive.

The temple is built almost entirely of laterite and sandstone and follows a typical Khmer layout: a central sanctuary, a surrounding gallery, a long causeway, and a large rectangular reservoir. The lintels feature fine carvings of Hindu deities, especially Shiva and Vishnu. Unlike the crowded temples of Angkor, Ta Muen Thom sees very few visitors – you may have the entire site to yourself.

Due to its location in a sensitive border area, you must check in with Cambodian military/police at a checkpoint a few kilometers before the temple. They will ask for your passport and may require a small donation or fee (around $5–10). It is also possible to visit from the Thai side (Surin province) via the Chong Chom border crossing, but crossing directly at the temple is not permitted – you must stay on the Cambodian side. The remote location and jungle setting give the temple an adventurous, Indiana‑Jones feel.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Explore the largest of the three Ta Muen temples
  • • See fine lintel carvings of Hindu mythology
  • • Enjoy the remote, jungle‑covered setting (almost no tourists)
  • • Visit nearby Ta Muen Toch and Ta Muen temples
  • • Combine with a trip to the Preah Vihear temple area

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Cross the border into Thailand at the temple site
  • • Climb on unstable or crumbling structures
  • • Litter or damage the carvings
  • • Visit without registering at the military checkpoint

💡 Pro tip: Hire a 4WD or motorbike from Anlong Veng (the nearest town) – the road is rough. Bring your passport. Expect to pay a small "donation" to the military checkpoint ($5–10). Go early morning to avoid afternoon heat and border patrol activity. The site is free aside from the donation.
