Cardamom Mountains

Southwest Cambodia (Koh Kong, Pursat, Kampong Speu)

📍 150 km southwest of Phnom Penh
☀️ Nov — Apr (Dry season)

Southeast Asia's Last Great Rainforest

The Cardamom Mountains (Chuor Phnom Krâvanh) form one of the largest and most biodiverse rainforests in mainland Southeast Asia. Spanning over 20,000 square kilometers, this mountain range stretches from the Gulf of Thailand inland, covering parts of Koh Kong, Pursat, Kampong Speu, and Battambang provinces.

Often called the "last great wilderness of Cambodia," the Cardamoms are home to an incredible array of rare and endangered wildlife: Asian elephants, clouded leopards, sun bears, pileated gibbons, Siamese crocodiles, and the highly elusive Indochinese tiger (if any remain). The forests also shelter hundreds of bird species, including the great hornbill and the endemic Cambodian laughingthrush.

The region is a paradise for eco‑tourists and adventure travelers. Activities include multi‑day jungle trekking, kayaking through mangrove forests, swimming under waterfalls (like Tatai and Chhay Borei), river cruises, and staying in community‑run eco‑lodges. Conservation projects allow visitors to participate in wildlife monitoring, tree planting, and supporting local indigenous communities.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Jungle trekking (half‑day to 5‑day expeditions)
  • • Kayaking on the Tatai River and mangrove forests
  • • Visit waterfalls – Tatai, Chhay Borei, Kbal Spean 2
  • • Stay at eco‑lodges (4 Rivers, Cardamom Tented Camp)
  • • Join conservation volunteering (wildlife monitoring, tree planting)

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Litter or damage the fragile forest ecosystem
  • • Hunt, trap, or disturb wildlife
  • • Enter restricted conservation zones without a guide
  • • Use drones without permission from local authorities

💡 Pro tip: The dry season (November–April) is best for trekking and kayaking. Base yourself in Koh Kong town or Chi Phat village – Chi Phat offers excellent community‑based eco‑tours. Always hire a local guide for safety and to support the community.
