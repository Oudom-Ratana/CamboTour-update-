Phnom Chisor Temple

Takeo Province, Cambodia

📍 45 km south of Phnom Penh (near the Vietnamese border)
🏛️ Built: 11th century (King Suryavarman I)

Hilltop Angkorian Sanctuary

Phnom Chisor Temple is an 11th‑century Angkorian temple located atop a 130‑meter limestone hill in Takeo province. Built during the reign of King Suryavarman I (1002–1050 AD), the temple is dedicated to Shiva and Vishnu. Its name means “High Mountain”, and the site offers spectacular panoramic views of the surrounding rice paddies, sugar palm trees, and the distant Vietnamese border.

To reach the sanctuary, visitors climb a long staircase of over 400 steps flanked by ancient laterite walls. At the top, you'll find the main prasat (sanctuary), several smaller towers, a long gallery, and a library. The carvings are well‑preserved, including delicate lintels depicting Hindu scenes. A modern pagoda sits near the ruins, and local monks often welcome visitors.

Phnom Chisor is much less crowded than the temples of Angkor, offering a peaceful, off‑the‑beaten‑path experience. It is an easy day trip from Phnom Penh (about 1.5 hours by car) and can be combined with a visit to the nearby Phnom Da temple or the Takeo silk weaving villages. The site is free to enter, but a small donation for the caretaker is appreciated.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Climb the 400+ steps for stunning hilltop views
  • • Explore the well‑preserved Hindu carvings and lintels
  • • Visit the modern pagoda and chat with monks
  • • Enjoy a peaceful, uncrowded Angkorian ruin
  • • Combine with a trip to Phnom Da or Takeo silk villages

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Wear revealing clothing (shoulders/knees covered)
  • • Climb on unstable or crumbling stone structures
  • • Litter or damage the carvings
  • • Fly drones without permission

💡 Pro tip: Go early morning (7–9 AM) to avoid the heat and enjoy the best light for photography. Bring water and wear sturdy shoes – the stairs are steep. A tuk‑tuk from Phnom Penh costs about $30–40 round trip. The site is free, but a small donation at the top is appreciated.
