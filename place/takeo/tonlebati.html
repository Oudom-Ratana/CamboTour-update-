Tonle Bati

Takeo Province, Cambodia

📍 35 km south of Phnom Penh (45‑min drive)
🏛️ Angkorian temples & scenic lake

A Popular Weekend Escape

Tonle Bati is a large, natural lake located about 35 kilometers south of Phnom Penh. It is a beloved weekend destination for Cambodian families, offering a peaceful mix of nature, history, and local recreation. The lake is surrounded by shady trees and features floating pavilions where visitors can enjoy fresh fish and other Khmer dishes while cooling their feet in the water.

At the southern edge of the lake stand two well‑preserved Angkorian temples: Ta Prohm (not to be confused with the famous Ta Prohm in Angkor) and Yeay Peau. These small brick sanctuaries date from the late 12th century (King Jayavarman VII) and feature intricate lintel carvings. Unlike the crowded temples of Siem Reap, here you can explore in relative peace.

The site also has a small zoo with deer and birds, picnic areas, and boat rentals. It’s an excellent spot for a half‑day trip from Phnom Penh, combining a bit of history with a relaxing lakeside meal. The entrance fee is minimal (around $1–2), and food from the floating restaurants is reasonably priced. Tonle Bati is especially lively during Khmer New Year and weekends.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Walk through the Ta Prohm and Yeay Peau temples
  • • Enjoy a meal on a floating pavilion (try the fried fish)
  • • Rent a swan paddle boat on the lake
  • • Picnic under the shade of large trees
  • • Visit the small zoo and see deer

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Swim in the lake (water quality is not safe)
  • • Litter – keep the lake clean
  • • Climb on the ancient temple carvings
  • • Feed the zoo animals without permission

💡 Pro tip: Visit on a weekday to avoid the weekend crowds. The lake is just 45 minutes from Phnom Penh by car or tuk‑tuk (about $15–20 one way). Allow 2–3 hours for the temples, lunch, and a paddle boat ride. Entrance fee is $1–2. Try the local specialty – grilled snakehead fish with Khmer herbs.
