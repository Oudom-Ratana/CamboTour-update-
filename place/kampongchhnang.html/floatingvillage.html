Kampong Chhnang Floating Village

Kampong Chhnang Province

📍 10 km east of Kampong Chhnang town
☀️ Year-round (best Nov–Mar)

Life on the Tonle Sap

Kampong Chhnang Floating Village is one of the most authentic and least-touristy floating communities in Cambodia. Located on the edge of the Tonle Sap Lake, the village consists of hundreds of homes, schools, churches, and even a floating basketball court – all built on stilts or floating platforms that rise and fall with the water level.

The area is also famous for traditional pottery. Kampong Chhnang means "Port of Pottery" – local families have been making clay pots, jars, and water storage vessels for centuries using techniques passed down through generations. You can visit workshops and watch artisans at work.

A boat tour takes you through narrow canals lined with floating homes, past fish farms, crocodile pens, and floating markets. The villagers are welcoming, and you'll see daily life unfolding on the water – children rowing to school in small boats, women washing clothes on their floating porches, and fishermen casting nets. It's a living water world that feels a world away from the modern cities.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Take a private boat tour through the floating village (1–2 hours)
  • • Visit traditional pottery workshops and try making a pot
  • • See floating schools, churches, and fish farms
  • • Buy handmade clay pots and local crafts
  • • Watch sunset over the Tonle Sap from a boat

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Swim – water is murky and has strong currents
  • • Litter – protect the fragile lake ecosystem
  • • Enter homes without invitation
  • • Use drones without asking local authorities

💡 Pro tip: Hire a boat from the dock near Kampong Chhnang town – expect $15–25 for a private tour. Go early morning (6–8 AM) to see daily life at its most active. Combine with a visit to Phnom Da or the pottery villages.
