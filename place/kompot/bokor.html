Bokor National Park

Kampot Province

📍 35 km west of Kampot city
☀️ Nov — Mar (Cool & dry)

Ghostly Hill Station & Pristine Rainforest

Bokor National Park covers over 1,500 square kilometers of dense montane rainforest atop the Elephant Mountains. The park's main attraction is the eerie, abandoned Bokor Hill Station, a French colonial resort built in the 1920s and abandoned during the Khmer Rouge era. Today, crumbling hotels, a ruined Catholic church, a royal residence, and the infamous "Black Palace" stand frozen in time, often shrouded in mist.

At over 1,000 meters above sea level, the summit offers breathtaking views of the Gulf of Thailand, Kampot town, and the surrounding jungle. On clear days, you can see the distant islands of Kep and even Vietnam. The park is home to gibbons, sun bears, clouded leopards, and over 300 bird species. A new luxury resort (Thansur) has been built, but most visitors come for the haunting ruins and cool mountain air.

Access is via a winding paved road (good condition) from Kampot. The park has several marked trails, waterfalls (Popokvil), and picnic areas. It can be visited as a day trip from Kampot or Kep. Be prepared for sudden weather changes – fog and rain are common even in dry season. Bring a jacket – it's much cooler than the coast.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Explore the abandoned French hill station ruins
  • • Visit the old Catholic church and the Black Palace
  • • Hike to Popokvil Waterfall (two tiers)
  • • Enjoy panoramic views of the Gulf of Thailand
  • • Watch for wildlife – gibbons, hornbills, monkeys

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Enter unstable or barricaded buildings
  • • Litter or damage the historical ruins
  • • Feed or disturb wildlife
  • • Swim in Popokvil waterfall (dangerous currents)

💡 Pro tip: Rent a motorbike or hire a tuk‑tuk from Kampot ($25–30 round trip, including waiting). Start early (8 AM) to avoid afternoon fog. Bring a jacket, water, and snacks – there are few shops on the mountain. The entry fee is about $5 per person.
