Kampot Pepper Plantation

Kampot Province

📍 10–20 km north/east of Kampot city
☀️ Year-round (harvest Feb–Jun)

The World's Finest Pepper

Kampot Pepper is globally renowned as some of the best pepper in the world. It has been awarded PGI (Protected Geographical Indication) status by the European Union, recognizing its unique terroir – a perfect blend of rich soil, sea breezes, and abundant sunshine. Grown using traditional, organic methods for over a century, Kampot pepper comes in four varieties: black, white, red, and green.

The pepper plantations are family‑owned farms nestled among rice paddies, coconut palms, and limestone karsts. Visiting one offers a glimpse into sustainable agriculture and the labor‑intensive craft of hand‑picking each peppercorn. You'll see the tall wooden stakes supporting climbing vines, learn how pepper is harvested, dried, and sorted, and – best of all – taste the difference: floral, eucalyptus‑tinged black pepper, creamy white pepper, and fruity, rare red pepper.

Several plantations welcome visitors for guided tours, including La Plantation (one of the largest and most visitor‑friendly), Sothy’s Pepper Farm (family‑run with a cooking demo), and Farm Link. Most tours include a walk through the vines, explanation of the growing cycle, and a generous tasting session. The farms are located in the countryside north of Kampot, making for a scenic half‑day excursion by motorbike or tuk‑tuk.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Tour a family‑run pepper farm (La Plantation, Sothy’s, Farm Link)
  • • See how black, white, red, and green pepper are produced
  • • Taste fresh peppercorns and buy organic pepper directly
  • • Learn about organic farming and PGI certification
  • • Enjoy countryside views of rice fields and karst mountains

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Pick peppercorns without permission
  • • Damage vines or stakes
  • • Litter or walk through restricted farm areas
  • • Bargain aggressively – prices are fair and support farmers

💡 Pro tip: La Plantation offers free tours in English/French at 10 AM, 1 PM, and 3 PM daily. The best time to visit is during harvest (February–June) when vines are heavy with peppercorns. Buy directly from the farm – it's cheaper and fresher than in town.
