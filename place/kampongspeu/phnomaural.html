Phnom Aural

Kampong Speu

📍 ~80 km west of Kampong Speu town
☀️ Dec — Apr (Dry season)

Cambodia's Highest Peak

Phnom Aural (also spelled Phnom Aoral) is the highest mountain in Cambodia, standing at 1,813 meters (5,948 feet) above sea level. It is part of the Cardamom Mountains – one of Southeast Asia's last remaining rainforest wildernesses. The peak lies on the border between Pursat and Kampong Speu provinces, within the Phnom Aural Wildlife Sanctuary.

The mountain is covered in dense evergreen forest, home to rare wildlife including Asian elephants, clouded leopards, sun bears, gibbons, and over 300 bird species. The climb to the summit is challenging but rewarding, offering panoramic views over the Cardamom range and, on a clear day, glimpses of the Gulf of Thailand.

Reaching the summit requires a multi‑day trek (typically 2–3 days) with a local guide. The trail passes through remote villages of the Chong and Por indigenous communities, rubber plantations, and steep jungle slopes. Because the area is still very wild, a permit and ranger escort are required – making it one of the most authentic and adventurous mountain experiences in mainland Southeast Asia.

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Multi‑day trek to the highest point in Cambodia
  • • Hire a local guide and ranger (mandatory for safety)
  • • Camp overnight in the jungle near waterfalls
  • • See rare wildlife and pristine montane forest
  • • Learn from indigenous Chong and Por communities

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Climb without a licensed guide and permit
  • • Litter or damage the protected forest
  • • Hunt, trap, or remove any plants or animals
  • • Go alone – the area is remote and has no mobile signal

💡 Pro tip: Plan for a 3‑day / 2‑night trek. Best time is dry season (December–April). Contact the Kampong Speu Provincial Department of Tourism or a reputable eco‑tour operator in Phnom Penh to arrange permits, guides, and transport.
