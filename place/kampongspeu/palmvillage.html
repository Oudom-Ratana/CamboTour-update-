Kampong Speu Palm Sugar Villages

Kampong Speu Province

📍 15–30 km north of Kampong Speu town
☀️ Year-round (best Nov–May)

Sweet Tradition of Cambodia

The Kampong Speu Palm Sugar Villages are the heart of Cambodia’s traditional palm sugar industry. For generations, local families have climbed sugar palm trees (Borassus flabellifer) to harvest the sweet sap, which is then boiled down into rich, aromatic sugar cakes. This ancient craft received GI (Geographical Indication) status from the Cambodian government, recognizing its unique quality and heritage.

Scattered across the rural countryside north of the provincial capital, villages like Oudong, Prey Kuy, and Chambok offer visitors a glimpse into this slow, sustainable lifestyle. You can watch men ascend tall palm trunks with bamboo ladders, collect the sap in clay pots, and then see women stir giant copper kettles over wood fires until the syrup crystallizes into golden-brown blocks of pure palm sugar.

The finished product has a complex caramel flavor with hints of vanilla and toffee – far superior to refined sugar. It is used in Cambodian desserts, marinades, and even as a natural sweetener for coffee. Visitors can buy fresh sugar cakes, palm wine, and handwoven baskets directly from the producers. The villages are also dotted with ancient pagodas and peaceful rice fields, making for a relaxing half‑day trip from Phnom Penh or Kampong Speu.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Watch palm sap harvesting and traditional boiling
  • • Taste fresh, warm palm sugar straight from the kettle
  • • Buy GI‑certified palm sugar cakes, palm wine, and crafts
  • • Visit nearby Oudong Mountain and ancient pagodas
  • • Cycle through sugar palm forests and rice paddies

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Climb the palm trees yourself (dangerous without training)
  • • Disturb the animals or damage the tree trunks
  • • Enter sugar‑boiling huts without permission
  • • Litter or leave plastic waste in the villages

💡 Pro tip: Visit in the morning (8–11 AM) when sugar boiling is active. The best villages are about 20 minutes north of Kampong Speu town. Hire a local guide or just ask friendly families – they are proud to show their craft.
