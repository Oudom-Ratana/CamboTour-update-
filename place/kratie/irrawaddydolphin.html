Irrawaddy Dolphins at Kampi

Kratie Province

📍 15 km north of Kratie town, along Mekong River
☀️ Year-round (best Nov–May, dry season)

Rare Mekong River Treasure

The Irrawaddy dolphin (Orcaella brevirostris) is one of the world's most endangered marine mammals, and the Mekong River population is critically threatened. Kampi village, north of Kratie, is the best place in Cambodia to see these beautiful, shy dolphins in their natural habitat. Only about 90 individuals remain in the Mekong, and Kampi is home to one of the last viable groups.

The dolphins have a distinctive rounded head (no beak) and a playful nature. They often surface gracefully, sometimes in pairs or small groups, to breathe. The best viewing is early morning (7–9 AM) or late afternoon (3–5 PM) when they are most active. You'll take a small local boat from Kampi village to a designated dolphin pool area. Boat operators are trained to approach slowly and respectfully, giving you a chance to observe without disturbing them.

Conservation efforts, including the Cambodian government's dolphin conservation zone and local community patrols, have helped stabilize the population. By visiting Kampi, you directly support these efforts – boat fees contribute to conservation and community development. Bring binoculars, a camera with a zoom lens, and patience. Seeing a dolphin break the glassy Mekong surface is an unforgettable experience.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Take a 1–2 hour boat trip to see Irrawaddy dolphins
  • • Watch from the observation platform (free, but limited view)
  • • Learn about dolphin conservation at the visitor center
  • • Combine with a visit to Kratie's 100 Pillar Pagoda
  • • Support local community by buying handicrafts

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Touch, feed, or swim with the dolphins
  • • Use loud engines or speed boats near the dolphin zone
  • • Litter or throw anything into the river
  • • Approach closer than 50 meters to the dolphins

💡 Pro tip: Go at 7 AM – fewer tourists, calmer water, and dolphins are most active. Boat costs $9 per person (official price). Negotiate only if you charter a whole boat. Bring sunscreen, a hat, and binoculars. Combine with a Mekong river trip to Koh Trong island.
