Sambor Prei Kuk

Kampong Thom Province

📍 25 km north of Kampong Thom city
☀️ Nov — Mar (Cool & Dry Season)

Pre‑Angkorian Masterpiece

Sambor Prei Kuk ("Temple in the rich forest") is the most important group of pre‑Angkorian monuments in Cambodia. Built in the early 7th century during the Chenla Kingdom, it predates Angkor Wat by more than 500 years. The site was inscribed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 2017.

The complex contains over 100 brick temples scattered through dense jungle, divided into three main groups: North (N16), Central (C1–C9), and South (S1–S4). The central group is the oldest and most impressive, featuring octagonal towers, intricate carved lintels depicting Hindu gods (especially Shiva and Vishnu), and a large man‑made pond.

Unlike the crowded temples of Angkor, Sambor Prei Kuk offers a peaceful, adventurous atmosphere. Ancient trees grow over the ruins, and the jungle setting makes you feel like an explorer. The site is still actively studied by archaeologists, and many areas remain unrestored, adding to its raw, authentic charm.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Explore three temple groups (North, Central, South)
  • • Admire rare 7th‑century octagonal brick towers
  • • See intricately carved lintels of Hindu deities
  • • Walk the jungle trails – home to birds and monkeys
  • • Hire a local guide to explain Chenla history

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Climb or lean on fragile brick structures
  • • Touch or remove any carvings or artifacts
  • • Wear inappropriate clothing (shoulders/knees covered)
  • • Litter or damage the forest environment

💡 Pro tip: Hire a motorbike or tuk‑tuk from Kampong Thom ($15–20 round trip). Allow at least 2–3 hours to explore. Go early morning to avoid heat and catch beautiful light filtering through the jungle.
