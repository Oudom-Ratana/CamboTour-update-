Phnom Santuk

Kampong Thom Province

📍 7 km south of Kampong Thom city
☀️ Year-round (best Nov–Mar)

Sacred Hill of Statues

Phnom Santuk (also called Santuk Mountain) is a prominent 200-meter-high hill known for its stunning collection of Buddhist statues. More than 100 life-sized Buddha figures – standing, sitting, and reclining – are scattered across the hillside, along with giant sandstone turtles, lions, and nagas. The site is an important pilgrimage destination for Cambodian Buddhists.

Visitors ascend 809 stone steps flanked by naga balustrades. Along the way, you'll pass pavilions with beautiful murals depicting scenes from the life of Buddha and Khmer legends. The climb is steep but rewarding – at the summit, a 10-meter-long reclining Buddha and a 12-meter-tall standing Buddha offer breathtaking views of the surrounding rice fields, palm trees, and the Stung Sen River.

Phnom Santuk is less crowded than other Cambodian religious sites, giving it a peaceful, contemplative atmosphere. The forested hill is also home to monkeys and birds, adding to the natural charm. Whether you're seeking spiritual solace or simply want to admire impressive Buddhist art, this sacred mountain is a highlight of Kampong Thom province.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Climb 809 stairs past dozens of Buddha statues
  • • See the giant reclining and standing Buddhas at the summit
  • • Enjoy panoramic views of Kampong Thom countryside
  • • Visit the pagoda and murals along the staircase
  • • Watch the sunset over the Stung Sen River

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Climb on the Buddha statues
  • • Wear revealing clothing (shoulders/knees covered)
  • • Feed or harass the monkeys
  • • Litter or damage the religious carvings

💡 Pro tip: Go early morning (6–8 AM) to avoid heat and crowds. Bring water and wear comfortable hiking shoes. The climb takes 30–45 minutes. Combine with a visit to Sambor Prei Kuk temple complex for a full day.
