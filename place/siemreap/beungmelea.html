Beng Mealea (បឹងមាលា)

Siem Reap Province, Cambodia

📍 40 km east of Angkor Wat (70 km from Siem Reap city)
🏛️ Built: mid‑12th century (Suryavarman II)

The Temple in the Jungle

Beng Mealea (meaning "lotus pond") is a sprawling, 12th‑century Angkorian temple built by King Suryavarman II, the same king who built Angkor Wat. Unlike its famous cousin, Beng Mealea has been left largely unrestored – trees and vines grow over collapsed stone towers, making it one of the most atmospheric and adventurous ruins in Cambodia.

The temple is built in the same architectural style as Angkor Wat, with a central sanctuary surrounded by three concentric enclosures, libraries, and a massive moat. However, much of the structure has collapsed, creating a maze of rubble, fallen lintels, and dark chambers. Visitors can scramble over rocks, walk along elevated wooden walkways (installed for safety), and experience the "Indiana Jones" feeling that is hard to find elsewhere.

Because it is located off the main tourist circuit (about 1.5 hours from Siem Reap), Beng Mealea is much less crowded than Angkor. It is a perfect complement to a visit to Koh Ker or a full‑day exploration of the more remote Angkorian sites. The temple is surrounded by jungle, and the sound of birds and rustling leaves adds to its mystical charm.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Explore the collapsed, unrestored jungle ruins
  • • Walk the wooden walkways through the central sanctuary
  • • Scramble over stone blocks (watch your step!)
  • • See stunning carvings of Hindu deities on fallen lintels
  • • Combine with a trip to Koh Ker or Phnom Kulen

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Climb on unstable or barricaded structures
  • • Touch or remove any carvings or artifacts
  • • Litter or damage the jungle environment
  • • Visit after dark (temple closes at 5:30 PM)

💡 Pro tip: Hire a car from Siem Reap ($45–55 round trip). The road is paved and good. Entry fee is included in some Angkor passes? Actually Beng Mealea requires a separate ticket (about $5–10). Bring sturdy shoes, water, and insect repellent. Go early morning (8–9 AM) to avoid heat and have the temple almost to yourself. Allow 1.5–2 hours for exploring.
