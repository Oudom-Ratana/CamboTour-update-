Pub Street (Walking Street)

Siem Reap, Cambodia

📍 City center, near Old Market (Psar Chas)
🍻 Open evenings (5 PM – late)

Heart of Siem Reap Nightlife

Pub Street is the epicenter of Siem Reap's nightlife and dining scene. Every evening from around 5 PM, the street closes to vehicles and transforms into a vibrant pedestrian zone filled with restaurants, bars, live music venues, nightclubs, and street food stalls. It's the place where tourists and locals come together to relax, eat, and celebrate after a day exploring the Angkor temples.

The atmosphere is electric but laid‑back. You'll find everything from traditional Khmer cuisine and barbecue to pizza, tacos, and Indian food. Many restaurants offer $1–3 draft beers, and happy hours are common. The street is lined with colorful neon signs, fairy lights, and outdoor seating. For the adventurous, try the famous "happy pizza" (clearly labeled) or deep‑fried insects from a street cart – crickets, tarantulas, and scorpions are surprisingly tasty.

Live music spills out of bars like Temple Bar, Angkor What? (the original backpacker bar), and YOLO Bar. Nightclubs like Phare Club and Ming Club keep the party going until late. If you prefer something quieter, side alleys offer chill lounges and craft beer spots. Pub Street is also just steps from the lively Old Market (Psar Chas), where you can shop for souvenirs, silk, and art before or after your meal. It's safe, walkable, and an essential Siem Reap experience.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Enjoy $0.50–$1 draft beers at happy hour (5–7 PM)
  • • Try Khmer barbecue, fried insects, or "happy pizza"
  • • Listen to live music at Temple Bar, Angkor What?, or YOLO Bar
  • • Dance at nightclubs like Phare Club or Ming Club
  • • Shop at the adjacent Old Market (Psar Chas) for souvenirs

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Bring outside alcohol into bars (support the venues)
  • • Litter or cause public disturbances
  • • Use illegal drugs (strict enforcement)
  • • Bargain aggressively at street food stalls (prices are already low)

💡 Pro tip: The street gets busy after 8 PM. Go around 6–7 PM for a more relaxed dinner and happy hour. For budget food, try the night market stalls just off Pub Street. Tuk‑tuks are everywhere – a ride back to most hotels costs $1–3. Be mindful of your belongings in crowded areas, but overall it's very safe.
