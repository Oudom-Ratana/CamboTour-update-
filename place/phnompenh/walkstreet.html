Phnom Penh Walking Street

Sisowath Quay & Riverside – Phnom Penh, Cambodia

📍 Sisowath Quay, from near Wat Phnom to the Royal Palace
📅 Saturday & Sunday evenings (6:00 PM – 11:00 PM)
🚶 Car‑free pedestrian zone on weekends

Riverside Promenade Turned Night Market

Since early 2025, Phnom Penh has launched a large‑scale weekend walking street along Sisowath Quay. Stretching approximately 1.5 kilometers from near Wat Phnom down to the Royal Palace area, the road is closed to vehicles on Saturday and Sunday evenings from 6:00 PM to 11:00 PM, transforming the riverfront into a lively pedestrian zone filled with food stalls, souvenir vendors, live music, and street performances.

Visitors can browse stalls selling traditional Khmer silk scarves (krama), wood carvings, silver jewelry, and handmade crafts. Food lovers will find everything from grilled squid and beef skewers to tropical fruit shakes and fried insects (crickets, silk worms, and even tarantulas for the adventurous). Small stages host traditional Apsara dance performances, modern music, and occasional fire‑twirling shows.

Beyond the main walking street, Phnom Penh offers several other lively lanes. Bassac Lane in the Tonle Bassac district is famous for its chic cocktail bars, craft beer pubs, and international restaurants. Langka Lane in BKK1 is a quieter alternative with Japanese eateries, wine bars, and laid‑back cafes. For a more local experience, the area around Orussey Market turns into a bustling night food market after dark, packed with BBQ stalls and noodle shops.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Stroll the car‑free riverside on a Saturday or Sunday evening
  • • Shop for krama scarves, silverware, and local crafts
  • • Taste grilled street food, fruit shakes, and fried insects
  • • Watch traditional Apsara dance or live music performances
  • • Explore Bassac Lane (cocktail bars) or Langka Lane (bistros & pubs)

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Litter – bins are available; keep the riverfront clean
  • • Drink alcohol from outside venues (support local businesses)
  • • Touch performers or interrupt street shows
  • • Leave valuables unattended – stay aware of your surroundings

💡 Pro tip: Arrive around 6:00–7:00 PM to catch the sunset over the Tonle Sap river before the street gets crowded. Wear comfortable walking shoes – the quay is long. Bargain politely at souvenir stalls (10–20% off is reasonable). For a quieter drink, walk 10 minutes to Bassac Lane or Langka Lane.
