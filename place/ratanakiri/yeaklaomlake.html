Yeak Laom Lake

Banlung, Ratanakiri Province, Cambodia

📍 5 km south of Banlung town
🌋 Volcanic crater lake | 4,000+ years old

Sacred Crater Lake of Ratanakiri

Yeak Laom Lake is a stunning volcanic crater lake located just a few kilometers from Banlung town. Formed over 4,000 years ago by a volcanic eruption, the lake is nearly perfectly circular, about 800 meters in diameter, and up to 50 meters deep. The water is remarkably clear and clean, with a unique emerald-blue color that changes slightly with the light.

The lake is sacred to the indigenous Tampuan and Jarai people, who believe it is home to powerful spirits. A shaded wooden path circles the entire lake (about 2.5 km), passing through dense jungle. There are several swimming areas with wooden platforms, a small visitor center, and picnic spots. The water is cool and refreshing – perfect for a swim after hiking the loop.

Yeak Laom is the most popular natural attraction in Ratanakiri, but it never feels overcrowded. It is a place of natural beauty, spiritual significance, and ecological importance. The surrounding forest is home to birds, butterflies, and small mammals. It's an easy half‑day trip from Banlung, and a must‑see for anyone visiting northeastern Cambodia.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Swim in the cool, clear crater lake (designated areas only)
  • • Walk the 2.5‑km jungle trail around the entire lake
  • • Visit the small visitor center to learn about indigenous culture
  • • Picnic at shaded tables near the lake
  • • Watch for birds, butterflies, and monkeys

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Use soap or shampoo in the lake (protect the ecosystem)
  • • Litter – carry all trash back with you
  • • Feed or disturb the monkeys
  • • Swim outside designated areas (the lake is deep)

💡 Pro tip: Go early morning (8–9 AM) to avoid crowds and get the best swimming conditions. Entry fee is about $2–3. Rent a motorbike in Banlung ($8–10/day) – the road is paved and easy. Bring a towel, water shoes (slippery rocks), and a waterproof bag for your phone. The walk around the lake takes 30–45 minutes.
