Virachey National Park

Ratanakiri & Stung Treng Provinces, Cambodia

📍 332,500 hectares (largest national park in Cambodia)
🌿 ASEAN Heritage Park (2003)

Cambodia's Untamed Wilderness

Virachey National Park is the largest protected area in Cambodia, covering over 332,500 hectares of pristine rainforest, grasslands, and river systems. It straddles the borders of Laos and Vietnam, forming part of one of Southeast Asia's most important biodiversity corridors. The park is a haven for endangered species including Asian elephants, gibbons, clouded leopards, sun bears, and the elusive Siamese crocodile.

The park is also home to several indigenous groups, including the Kreung, Tampuan, and Lao peoples, who maintain traditional lifestyles. Community‑based ecotourism allows visitors to trek with local guides, sleep in homestays, and learn about forest survival skills. The park's remote location means very few tourists visit, offering a genuine off‑the‑grid adventure.

Activities include multi‑day jungle treks, river trips to see waterfalls, wildlife spotting, and visiting ethnic minority villages. The best base for exploring Virachey is the small town of Ban Lung (Ratanakiri) or Veun Sai. Permits and mandatory local guides are required, and treks range from one to seven days. This is not a casual day trip – it's for serious nature lovers and adventurers.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Multi‑day jungle trekking (1–7 days) with local guides
  • • River trips to waterfalls and remote villages
  • • Wildlife spotting – gibbons, elephants, hornbills
  • • Stay in indigenous homestays (Kreung, Tampuan)
  • • Learn forest survival skills and traditional hunting methods

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Enter the park without a licensed guide and permit
  • • Litter or damage the fragile ecosystem
  • • Hunt, trap, or remove any plants or animals
  • • Go alone – the park is vast and has no phone signal

💡 Pro tip: Book through a responsible tour operator in Ban Lung (e.g., Virachey National Park Eco‑tourism Office). Permits cost about $15–20, plus guide fees ($15–25/day). The dry season (November–April) is best for trekking. Bring insect repellent, a good sleeping bag, and a water filter. Be prepared for leeches, steep trails, and basic facilities – the reward is pure, wild Cambodia.
