Preah Vihear Temple (Prasat Preah Vihear)

Preah Vihear Province, Cambodia

📍 Dângrêk Mountains, 140 km northwest of Siem Reap
🏛️ UNESCO World Heritage Site (2008)

Dramatic Cliff‑top Khmer Sanctuary

Preah Vihear Temple is one of the most spectacular and least‑visited Angkorian monuments in Cambodia. Built between the 9th and 12th centuries by several Khmer kings, the temple sits atop a 525‑meter cliff in the Dângrêk Mountains, directly on the Cambodian‑Thai border. Its dramatic location and stunning architecture earned it UNESCO World Heritage status in 2008.

Unlike most Khmer temples which are built on a flat axis, Preah Vihear is arranged along a north‑south axis, with a long causeway nearly 800 meters long leading to the main sanctuary. Visitors climb through five monumental gopuras (gateways), each offering increasingly breathtaking views of the plains below. The sanctuary itself is dedicated to the Hindu god Shiva, with exquisite sandstone carvings of deities, nagas, and floral motifs.

Because of its remote location and history of border conflict, Preah Vihear sees a fraction of the visitors of Angkor Wat. The journey is an adventure – a winding road up the mountain, then a climb of hundreds of steps or a vehicle shuttle to the top. The reward is panoramic views of the Cambodian jungle and the Thai lowlands, and the thrill of exploring a virtually crowd‑free masterpiece of Khmer architecture.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Walk the 800‑meter causeway through five gopuras
  • • Admire the cliff‑edge views over Cambodia and Thailand
  • • See intricate sandstone carvings of Shiva and Hindu mythology
  • • Explore the remote, uncrowded sanctuary (few tourists)
  • • Visit the onsite museum and border monument

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Cross the border into Thailand at the temple
  • • Climb on unstable or crumbling structures
  • • Litter or damage the carvings
  • • Use drones without permission

💡 Pro tip: Hire a 4WD from Siem Reap or Phnom Penh – the road is winding and steep. Bring your passport (military checkpoint). Go early morning to avoid heat and afternoon clouds. The site has a vehicle shuttle from the base to the top ($5 round trip) – walking the stairs is strenuous but rewarding. Entry fee is about $10–12.
