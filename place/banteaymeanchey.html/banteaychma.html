Banteay Chhmar Temple

Thma Puok District, Banteay Meanchey Province

📍 60 km north of Battambang city
☀️ Nov — Mar (Cool Season)

History & Architecture

Banteay Chhmar is one of the most significant and least-visited Angkorian temples in Cambodia. Built in the late 12th century by King Jayavarman VII, it was a grand Buddhist temple complex dedicated to his son and four generals who died defending the Khmer Empire against the Chams.

The temple is famous for its massive face towers (similar to those at Bayon in Angkor Thom), intricate bas-reliefs depicting military processions and daily life, and a stunning 32-meter-long carving of Avalokiteshvara with 22 arms. The site covers nearly 2 square kilometers, making it one of the largest Angkorian complexes outside of Siem Reap.

Unlike crowded tourist sites, Banteay Chhmar remains largely overgrown and atmospheric, with jungle vines weaving through crumbling stone galleries. Local communities live among the ruins, adding a unique, authentic feel. Recent restoration efforts by the Global Heritage Fund have helped preserve its treasures.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Explore the massive face towers and bas-reliefs
  • • See the 22-armed Avalokiteshvara carving
  • • Walk through jungle-covered ruins away from crowds
  • • Visit the nearby Banteay Chhmar Marigold Farm
  • • Stay overnight in a local homestay

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Climb or lean on fragile stone structures
  • • Touch or remove any carvings or artifacts
  • • Enter restricted restoration zones
  • • Use drones without prior permission
  • • Visit after dark (temple closes at sunset)

💡 Pro tip: Hire a local guide from the community for $10–15 – they share incredible stories and support conservation. Bring water and snacks as facilities are limited.
