Phnom Yat (ភ្នំយ៉ាត)

Pailin Province

📍 In the center of Pailin city (Phsar Pailin area)
☀️ Year-round (best Nov–Mar cool season)

Sacred Hill & Gem of the Northwest

Phnom Yat (also called Wat Phnom Yat) is a small, sacred hill in the heart of Pailin city, the capital of Pailin Province. Rising only about 100 meters, it is nevertheless the most important religious and cultural site in the region. At the summit sits a beautifully decorated pagoda, a large reclining Buddha, and several other Buddhist statues, all shaded by mature trees. The hill is also the site of a small museum displaying artefacts from the Khmer Rouge period and local gem‑mining history.

Pailin is famous for its rubies, sapphires, and gemstones, and Phnom Yat has old gem pits at its base. Visitors can see small‑scale gem mining still happening today, and there are shops selling rough and cut stones at the foot of the hill. The hill is believed to be the home of the local guardian spirit "Yat" – a powerful neak ta who protects the city and its gem wealth. Local people come to pray for good luck in mining, business, and health.

The climb to the top involves a long staircase with naga balustrades, passing small shrines, a large gong, and a scenic viewpoint overlooking Pailin city and the surrounding Cardamom Mountains. The pagoda complex is peaceful, clean, and well‑maintained. It's an excellent place to learn about Pailin's unique history – as a former Khmer Rouge stronghold, as a gem‑mining boomtown, and now as a quiet provincial capital. The hill is free to visit, and you can easily walk from the town center.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Climb the staircase to the pagoda and reclining Buddha
  • • Enjoy panoramic views of Pailin city and the Cardamom Mountains
  • • Visit the small museum (Khmer Rouge history & gem mining)
  • • See gem pits and buy rubies/sapphires from local shops
  • • Make an offering at the neak ta shrine for good luck

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Wear revealing clothing (shoulders/knees covered)
  • • Touch or climb on the Buddha statues
  • • Litter or damage the religious structures
  • • Buy gemstones from unlicensed sellers (buy from reputable shops)

💡 Pro tip: Visit late afternoon (4–5 PM) to catch the golden light and cooler temperatures. The hill is a 10‑minute walk from the Pailin bus station. Gem shops at the base are happy to explain the mining process – but be cautious with very cheap "rubies". The hill is free, but a small donation at the pagoda is appreciated.
