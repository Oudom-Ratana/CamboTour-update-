Ba Phnom

Prey Veng Province, Cambodia

📍 45 km southeast of Prey Veng town
☀️ Year-round (best Nov–Mar)

Ancient Hill of Legend & Spirituality

Ba Phnom (also known as Phnom Ba) is a small but historically and spiritually significant hill in Prey Veng province. Rising just 35 meters above the surrounding rice plains, it is considered one of the most sacred sites in southeastern Cambodia. The hill is associated with the legendary founding of the Kingdom of Funan – according to ancient Chinese records, a foreign merchant named Kaundinya married a local queen, Soma (a nāga princess), at this location, marking the birth of the first Khmer kingdom.

Today, Ba Phnom is a active Buddhist pilgrimage site. The hill is topped with a modern pagoda, several stupas, and a large Buddha statue. The climb involves a shaded staircase lined with naga balustrades, passing small shrines and offering stations. Local people come to pray for good luck, health, and success, especially during Khmer New Year and other festivals. The site also contains a few pre‑Angkorian brick temple ruins and sandstone carvings, though many are heavily weathered.

The hill offers peaceful views over endless rice paddies, palm trees, and traditional wooden houses. It is rarely visited by foreign tourists, making it an authentic, off‑the‑beaten‑path experience. A small museum near the base displays Funan‑era artifacts, including pottery and statues found in the area. The site is easily reached by motorbike or car from Prey Veng town, and admission is free (donations welcome).

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • Climb the shaded staircase to the hilltop pagoda
  • • See pre‑Angkorian brick ruins and sandstone carvings
  • • Visit the small Funan museum near the base
  • • Enjoy panoramic views of rice paddies and palm trees
  • • Make an offering at the shrines for good luck

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Wear revealing clothing (shoulders/knees covered)
  • • Touch or damage the ancient carvings
  • • Litter or disturb the monks and worshippers
  • • Climb on the stupas or Buddha statues

💡 Pro tip: The site is free, but a small donation at the pagoda is appreciated. Go early morning (7–9 AM) to avoid heat. It's an easy climb – suitable for all ages. The museum may be closed on Mondays. Combine with a visit to the nearby Neak Loeung ferry or the Cambodian‑Vietnamese border markets.
