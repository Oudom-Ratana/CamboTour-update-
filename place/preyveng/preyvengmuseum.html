Prey Veng Provincial Museum

Prey Veng Town, Cambodia

📍 Prey Veng city center, near the market
🏛️ Opened in 2010

Funan & Chenla Treasures

Prey Veng Provincial Museum houses an impressive collection of artifacts from the pre‑Angkorian and Angkorian periods, with a special focus on the ancient Kingdom of Funan (1st–6th century). The museum was established in 2010 with support from the French government and is located in a traditional Khmer‑style building in the heart of Prey Veng town.

Highlights include sandstone statues of Hindu deities (Vishnu, Shiva, and Ganesha), bronze Buddha figures, ancient pottery, and a remarkable collection of Funan‑era jewelry and glass beads excavated from nearby sites such as Ba Phnom and Phnom Da. The museum also displays a replica of a 6th‑century inscription and a collection of Khmer Rouge–era weapons and documents, providing a full picture of the province's long history.

The museum is small but well‑organized, with English and Khmer labels. It is rarely crowded, offering a quiet, educational experience. A visit can be combined with a trip to the nearby Ba Phnom archaeological site (about 45 minutes away). The museum is open daily except Mondays, and admission is free (donations appreciated). It is an excellent stop for history enthusiasts traveling between Phnom Penh and the Vietnamese border.

Visitor Guide

✓ Things You Can Do

  • • See pre‑Angkorian sandstone statues and Funan jewelry
  • • Learn about the history of Funan and Chenla kingdoms
  • • View Khmer Rouge–era artifacts and documents
  • • Combine with a visit to Ba Phnom archaeological site
  • • Enjoy a quiet, uncrowded museum experience

✗ Things You Can't

  • • Touch the artifacts
  • • Use flash photography (may damage old pieces)
  • • Bring food or drinks inside
  • • Visit on Mondays (museum closed)

💡 Pro tip: Admission is free – a small donation at the entrance is welcome. Plan for 30–45 minutes. The museum is air‑conditioned, making it a good break from the heat. Staff are friendly and may offer a short tour in English. Open Tuesday–Sunday, 8 AM – 11:30 AM and 2 PM – 5 PM.
